Melemele Meadow
Melemele Meadow
Melemele Meadow
is a secluded place where bright, yellow flowers grow in abundance. Wild Pokémon can be found among the blooms, while elevated earthen walkways facilitate travel through the area.

...and Found
Lillie
can be found near the meadow entrance. It turns out that she had taken off in pursuit of
Nebby
after its latest escape attempt. The Pokémon ran off through the flowers, but can be seen near the west side of the area. Speak to Lillie again before running off to have your Pokémon healed.
Follow the dirt path southward to reach a
Great Ball
and enter the first flower patch. During nighttime hours, a sparkling spot can be found here; inspect it to find a bit of
Yellow Nectar
. Cross westward through the flowers and up the ramp, then pick up the
Net Ball
to the south. Follow the dirt path to the northeast and jump off the ledge near the male Sightseer to reach the second flower patch. Head west and climb up the hill, past Nebby. Cross through the flowers here to reach a
Poison Barb
, and a Photo Spot at the end of the path. Go back past Nebby and through the flower patch to the east, pass by the female Sightseer, and jump the ledge. Head southwest through the first flower patch and inspect the wall to find a hidden tunnel to
Seaward Cave
.
Seaward Cave
Seaward Cave
Seaward Cave
connects
Melemele Meadow
and
Kala'e Bay
. The path slopes downward to water level where it opens to the bay. It is filled with the salty smell of the sea.

Kala'e Bay
Kala'e Bay
Kala'e Bay
is regarded as one of the most scenic spots on
Melemele Island
. Though the area is accessible, it cannot be fully explored at the moment. Trainers with the ability to
cross the water
will be able to explore the small islands and the southwest beach. A
Fishing Rod
will be necessary to encounter several of the marine species here.
